Hi, I'm Shubham. Thanks for going through and seeing what interests me. I have tried to distill my interests in this page, but if there's anything you'd like to know more about feel free to reach out.

Who am I?

I am a third year at the University of Pennsylvania studying Computer Science. Solving interesting problems and building is what excites me. These scenarios happen eveywhere from optimizing my Fortnite strategy to building my own journal system because I couldn't use google docs anymore. I like to call myself a life long learner and am always seeking out challenges. A quote I love: "In times of change, learners inherit the earth, while the learned find themselves beautifully equipped to deal with a world that no longer exists." - Eric Hoffer.

When I'm not glued to my computer, I love getting active and playing sports. Pickleball, Baskebtall, Football, you name it I'll play it. This also applies to board games and talking games. I love Avalon, Coup, Catan, Poker ( yes, even bomb pot double board PLO or banana), and any game that isn't pure luck (I'll never play roulette unless I'm the bank lol). I also have started reading more sci-fi and philosophy. Will be writing my thoughts on those and posting them somewhere soon.

Projects in the Pipeline:

Vibe coding is probably the most fun thing to do right now. I am building random things like website versions of games my friends and I love or random little apps perfectly tailored for my uses.

I know it might seem like a fad, but Polymarket is so interesting. I have been just trying random stuff out and seeing what happens. I am working on an openclaw autonomous trading agent competition of 4 instances to see which one will make the most money and just random ML projects based off polymarket data.

I am also trying to learn some more about robotics and that whole space. I am thinking of making some random arduino projects to make my life easier and just go back to my robotics roots. An idea I have is for a little machine that will auto unlock my house door when I am near and lock it when I leave a certain radius.

What drives me?

I love building things that people actually use (apps that only I will ever use count too lol). There's something deeply satisfying about taking an idea from a whiteboard sketch to a deployed product that solves a real problem. I also love wearing multiple hats and learning the ins and outs of different technologies.

I also believe the best way to learn is by doing. "I get the math behind ML but there's so many different models, how do I choose what to use?" Try them out. Find a real question you want to answer and see what works and think about why one might be better than another. By actually building, I can see where it is applicable, where it's not and how it works with other things I have learned. And hey, odds are I am building something that improves my quality of life so thats always a plus.
