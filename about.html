About me

Learn more about my projects and my journey as an engineer and builder.

Hi, I'm Shubham Kamdar. Thanks for going through my website and learning about me. I have tried my best to distill my interests in this page but if you have more questions feel free to reach out.

Who am I?

I'm a software engineer and builder based out of the US. I've always been drawn to the intersection of technology and problem-solving — whether that's building tools that make people's lives easier, exploring new paradigms in AI, or figuring out how financial markets tick.

I studied computer science and have spent the last few years working at startups, shipping products, and learning from some incredibly talented people. My time as a founding engineer at Oncore AI taught me what it takes to build from zero to one — and the acquisition by Perceptra (backed by General Catalyst) was a defining moment in my career.

Outside of work, you'll find me at the poker table, diving into a good sci-fi novel, or tinkering with some side project that caught my attention.

What am I working on?

Right now I'm exploring a few different areas that excite me. I've been building a Polymarket backtest engine to test trading strategies on prediction markets, working on multiplayer card games that I play with my friends, and maintaining a personal journal service that I built from scratch.

I'm also deeply interested in the AI agent space — particularly where it intersects with robotics and real-world applications. The idea of autonomous systems that can reason and act in physical environments is something I think about a lot.

What drives me?

I love building things that people actually use. There's something deeply satisfying about taking an idea from a whiteboard sketch to a deployed product that solves a real problem. I'm drawn to projects where I can wear multiple hats — from system design and backend architecture to crafting the frontend experience.

I also believe in learning by doing. Most of what I know has come from shipping things, breaking things, and figuring out how to fix them. Whether it was building Roomies and getting 200+ users or winning LA Hacks with HydroClick, the best lessons have always come from the projects themselves.

Get in touch

If you want to chat about anything — whether it's a project idea, a collaboration, or just to say hi — feel free to reach out on LinkedIn or check out my work on GitHub.
